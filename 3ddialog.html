The Configure 3D board dialog

This dialog allows you to set up the quality of the 3D board view.

The 'Rendering quality' options allow you to specify how the 3D models of the pieces are drawn, this will affect the speed at which CompuChess runs as some rendering options are faster than others.

  • Smooth shading- also known as 'Gouraud shading' this option is the slowest but the best looking, each model is shaded smoothly to make the models look realistic.
  • Flat shading- this option does not look as good as smooth shading but is faster, each model is shaded, but the shading is faceted rather than smooth.
  • Unlit flat shading- the models are not lit at all which means they do not have any shading.
  • Wireframe- the models have no texture applied and are represented by a wireframe mesh.

    The 'Enable textures' checkbox toggles whether or not the 3D models have textures applied, if not then they are shaded black or white.
